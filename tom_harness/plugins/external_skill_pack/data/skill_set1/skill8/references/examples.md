# UO-01 Reference

## Decision Variable

- The key variable is the character-specific appraisal override, not the default everyday emotional script.

## Route Signal

- Use this skill when the question asks which emotion fits after checking whether a trait, goal, value, or relationship overrides the generic response.

## Hard Boundary

- If the task asks why the surprising emotion happened, route to `skill9`.
- If there is no meaningful override cue and the story is just a standard reaction, do not force this skill.

## Shortcut To Avoid

- Do not answer from average-person intuition alone.
- Do not let threat words force fear or betrayal words force resentment before checking character-specific cues.

## Common Failure Modes

- Choosing a broad default emotion before examining override evidence.
- Missing moral self-appraisal cues that support guilt, regret, or embarrassment.
- Ignoring role, relationship history, or prior arrangement that changes the emotional meaning of the event.

## Minimal Pair

- Case A: an ordinary person hears footsteps at night and feels fear.
- Case B: a brave, curiosity-driven person in the same setup may feel curiosity instead.

## Boundary Stress Test

- `What emotion does she have?` with a strong override cue -> this skill.
- `She should feel betrayed, but why does she feel regret?` -> `skill9`.

## Generalization Note

- The transferable core is appraisal-based emotion reasoning: emotion follows how the character construes the event, not just the event itself.
