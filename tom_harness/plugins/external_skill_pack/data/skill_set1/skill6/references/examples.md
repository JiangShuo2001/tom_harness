# FB-04 Reference

## Decision Variable

- The key variable is the observer's inferred belief from the limited visible cue, not the sender's true motive.

## Route Signal

- Use this skill when the question targets what the observer thinks, feels, or does after seeing an ambiguous cue.

## Hard Boundary

- If the question asks why the sender made the cue, route to `skill7`.
- If there is no cue-interpretation step and the task is just direct emotion selection, do not use this skill.

## Shortcut To Avoid

- Do not answer with the sender's private intention.
- Do not import narrator knowledge the observer does not have.
- Do not default to a positive reading when the cue plausibly signals exclusion, threat, or mockery.

## Common Failure Modes

- Switching from observer reaction to sender intention.
- Treating a smile or glance as generic friendliness without checking context.
- Ignoring that the same cue can support suspicion, embarrassment, hurt, or confusion depending on local evidence.

## Minimal Pair

- Case A: the observer sees a private smile between others and feels excluded.
- Case B: the same smile is reassuring when the observer knows the shared plan.

## Boundary Stress Test

- `Why did she wink at him?` -> `skill7`.
- `How did he feel when he saw the wink?` -> this skill.

## Generalization Note

- The transferable core is perspective-taking from limited social evidence: what can the observer reasonably infer from what they saw?
