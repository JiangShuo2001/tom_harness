# AS-01 Reference

## Decision Variable

- The key variable is the sender's immediate social goal behind the cue.

## Route Signal

- Use this skill when the task asks why the cue sender smiled, glanced, winked, nudged, or otherwise signaled nonverbally.

## Hard Boundary

- If the task asks how another person interpreted the cue, route to `skill6`.
- If the task is about spoken indirect meaning rather than a nonverbal cue, route to `skill12`.

## Shortcut To Avoid

- Do not answer with the receiver's feeling.
- Do not treat the cue as a mood label without asking what concrete coordination or interpersonal goal it serves.

## Common Failure Modes

- Restating the cue instead of naming its purpose.
- Answering with `happy` or `friendly` when the real issue is warning, inviting, selecting, coordinating, or teasing.
- Ignoring shared background between sender and receiver.

## Minimal Pair

- Case A: a nod and smile silently mean `yes, go ahead`.
- Case B: the same expression is merely friendly when no shared plan exists.

## Boundary Stress Test

- `Why did he nudge her?` -> this skill.
- `How did she feel after he nudged her?` -> `skill6`.

## Generalization Note

- The transferable core is intention reconstruction from sparse social signals: what interpersonal goal best explains the cue in context?
